How to Use This Pet Insurance Calculator

Enter your pet's details and your preferred coverage options to get an estimated monthly premium. Our calculator uses average rate data from major pet insurance providers.

  • Pet Type & Breed — Dogs typically cost 2-3x more to insure than cats. Larger breeds have higher premiums due to increased health risks.
  • Pet Age — Premiums increase as your pet ages. Enrolling early locks in lower rates and avoids pre-existing condition exclusions.
  • Coverage Level — Accident-only is cheapest but doesn't cover illnesses. Comprehensive plans include wellness and preventive care.
  • Deductible & Reimbursement — Higher deductibles and lower reimbursement rates reduce your monthly premium.

Is Pet Insurance Worth It?

  • Average emergency vet visit: $800 - $1,500
  • Surgery (e.g., ACL repair): $2,000 - $5,000
  • Cancer treatment: $5,000 - $10,000+
  • Monthly premium: $20 - $70 for most dogs
  • Pet insurance can save you thousands on unexpected vet bills and give you peace of mind when making healthcare decisions for your pet.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does pet insurance cost?

Dog insurance typically costs $20-$70/month and cat insurance $10-$40/month. Factors include breed, age, location, and coverage level. Purebred dogs and certain breeds with known health issues cost more.

What does pet insurance cover?

Accident & illness plans cover unexpected injuries, surgeries, diagnostics, medications, and hospital stays. Wellness add-ons cover routine care like vaccinations, dental cleanings, and flea/heartworm prevention. Pre-existing conditions are generally excluded.

When should I get pet insurance?

The best time is when your pet is young and healthy. Premiums are lower, and you won't have pre-existing conditions excluded. Many providers offer coverage starting at 6-8 weeks of age.
