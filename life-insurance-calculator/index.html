How Much Life Insurance Do You Need?

The right amount depends on your income, debts, number of dependents, and financial goals. Our calculator factors in income replacement, outstanding debts, education costs, and final expenses, then subtracts your existing savings and coverage.

Term vs Whole Life Insurance

  • Term life — Covers you for a set period (10-30 years). More affordable, ideal for young families.
  • Whole life — Covers your entire life and builds cash value. Costs 5-15x more than term.
  • Recommendation — Most families are better served by term life and investing the difference.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much life insurance do I need?

A common rule is 10-12 times your annual income, plus outstanding debts. Our calculator gives a personalized number based on your specific situation including dependents, education costs, and existing resources.

What is the difference between term and whole life insurance?

Term life covers you for a set period (10-30 years) and is more affordable. Whole life covers your entire life and builds cash value but costs significantly more.

When should I buy life insurance?

The best time is when you're young and healthy, as premiums increase with age. If you have dependents, debts, or a mortgage, you should have coverage.
