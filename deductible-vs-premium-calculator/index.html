High vs Low Deductible: Which Is Better?

There's no one-size-fits-all answer. It depends on your expected claims, emergency savings, and risk tolerance. Use this calculator to see the actual numbers for your situation.

How to Choose the Right Deductible

  • Choose high deductible if: You have emergency savings, rarely file claims, and want lower monthly costs
  • Choose low deductible if: You prefer predictable costs, expect to file claims, or lack savings
  • Break-even point: If you file more claims than the break-even threshold, the lower deductible saves money

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I choose a high or low deductible?

Choose a high deductible if you have emergency savings and want lower premiums. Choose a low deductible if you prefer predictable costs and can afford higher premiums.

What is the break-even point for deductibles?

It's when the premium savings from a higher deductible equal the extra deductible cost if you file a claim. Our calculator computes this for you.

How do deductibles work with multiple claims?

Auto and home deductibles apply per claim. Health insurance typically has an annual deductible that resets each year.
